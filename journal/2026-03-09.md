### 7. Multi-Model Blending Architecture

Complete rewrite of `src/signals/model_blender.py` to support 4-way ensemble blending.

**Weight structure:**
- 4 global ensembles share `w_global` lead-time-adaptive weight curve
- Within `w_global`, proportional split: GFS 30%, ECMWF 30%, ICON-EPS 22%, GEM 18%
- Missing models have their share redistributed proportionally to available models
- HRRR weight still decays from 45% (0h) to 5% (48h+)
- Result clamped to [0.01, 0.99]

**Graceful degradation:** If only GFS and ECMWF are available (ICON-EPS and GEM stale), GFS and ECMWF each get 50% of `w_global` (30/(30+30) = 50%). The system never crashes or produces NaN probabilities regardless of which models are missing.

## Architecture Decisions

### Why proportional weight splitting instead of separate weight curves?

All 4 global ensembles provide fundamentally the same type of information (probabilistic temperature distributions), so they naturally compete for the same "share" of the blend. Giving each its own independent weight curve would create 4 additional hyperparameters to tune. The proportional split (30/30/22/18) reflects relative model quality and ensemble size while keeping the system simple.

### Why different minimum member thresholds per model?

GFS requires 20/31, ECMWF 25/51, ICON-EPS 20/40, GEM 10/21. The thresholds are roughly "enough members that the sample distribution is representative." Smaller ensembles (GEM) need a lower absolute threshold but represent a higher fraction of their total members.

### Why gem_global and not gem or gem_ensemble?

Open-Meteo's ensemble API accepts specific model names. Testing revealed: `gem` -> 400 error, `gem_seamless` -> only 1 member returned, `gem_global` -> full 21-member ensemble. The correct model parameter is `gem_global`.
